The Last Glow of Lumina

In 2157, Lieutenant Elara Voss follows a faint interstellar signal to Planet Lumina and discovers that saving humanity may mean protecting a living world from humanity itself.

In 2157, Earth’s resources were nearly exhausted, and the Interstellar Exploration Corps sent countless spaceships to search for habitable planets. Lieutenant Elara Voss, a young but experienced astronaut, piloted the Aurora alone on a mission to a remote galaxy, chasing a faint, unknown energy signal that might hold the key to humanity’s survival.

After a six-month warp journey, the Aurora emerged from hyperspace and docked in the orbit of Planet Lumina, a small, azure world covered in dense, bioluminescent forests. The planet’s atmosphere was breathable, a miracle Elara could hardly believe. She put on her lightweight exploration suit and descended to the surface in a shuttle.

What greeted her was a breathtaking sight. Giant, crystal-like trees emitted soft blue and purple light, floating flowers drifted through the air, and tiny, glowing creatures skittered across the mossy ground. The air was sweet, and the planet hummed with a gentle, rhythmic vibration, like a living heartbeat. Elara collected soil and plant samples, her excitement growing—this planet was perfect for human colonization.

But as she ventured deeper into the forest, she noticed something strange. The bioluminescent glow was fading in patches, the trees turning gray and brittle. Following the dimmed trail, she found a small, injured creature lying on the ground. It was about the size of a cat, with translucent wings and a core that glowed faintly, like a dying star. Beside it lay a broken, glowing crystal, the source of the energy signal she’d tracked.

Elara knelt down, her heart aching. She gently touched the creature, and a flood of images flooded her mind: Lumina was not just a planet, but a sentient being. The glowing crystals were its life force, and the small creatures were its guardians. Human probes that had passed through the galaxy decades earlier had damaged the planet’s core, slowly draining its energy. The creature was sending out a distress signal, not a call for colonization, but a plea for help.

Tears stung Elara’s eyes. She realized humanity’s greed would destroy this gentle, living world. She pulled out her communication device, ready to report back to Earth—but she paused. If she told them about Lumina’s habitability, ships would arrive within a year, strip the planet of its resources, and kill it.

Instead, she activated the shuttle’s self-destruct program, erasing all her navigation data. She took out a repair tool from her suit, carefully mending the broken crystal with the ship’s residual energy. The creature’s glow brightened, and it nuzzled her hand before fluttering back into the forest.

As the Aurora prepared to leave, Elara looked back at Lumina, its glow slowly returning. She transmitted a false report to Earth, claiming the planet was toxic and uninhabitable. She would carry this secret forever, choosing to protect a fragile alien world over humanity’s short-sighted survival.

When the Aurora jumped into hyperspace, Elara smiled. She knew humanity might find another way to survive, but Lumina’s light—pure, alive, and precious—would shine on, untouched by the greed of the stars.
