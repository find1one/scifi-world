[YILO]

[Todd Zhang0823]

When Yilo first learned the term aerial root, a familiar, strange feeling he had felt once struck him.

His teacher described them as roots that grew above ground, absorbing moisture from the air. The whole class copied notes without interest. Except Yilo, he stared at the diagram and thought of the pale roots that surfaced all across Alpha, even in the desert where he lived in.

He had felt this before at eight years old, the morning he realized his family had become abnormal. Nothing apparent had changed. Yet he could just find something essential had broken. Two months later he announced that he could not tolerate the abnormal atmosphere and pretend it’s still a family. He asked to be sent to his grandparents’ home. As long as he had seen the crack, he could no longer pretend the world are still in whole shape.

At that moment he saw another.

The roots of the Revelation Tree are said to surfaced everywhere humans lived. Temples were built around them. Priests said the Tree loved mankind so much that it extended itself even into the desert, helping them survive. But Yilo could not believe that.

A tree sought water and nutrients to grow, bloom and bear fruit. That’s the nature of plants. Even if its divinity is believed, it’s still unnatural for it to grow up here. Speaking of blooming and bearing fruit, there is no record about that. Then what is the purpose of the tree?

The ecology knowledge he learnt reminded him of a shocking idea: Human are actually also competitors of the Tree. Our existence is a competition for Tree’s resource. Why would it help us?

Why would a tree help its competitors live?

That question became an obsession. Yilo began comparing old maps, settlement records, temple dates. Slowly a pattern emerged. The roots did not appear first and attract people. People settled, and then roots arrived. New districts gained pale threads within months. Abandoned places lost them. The Tree was not blessing human life.

It was following it and even controlling it.

From that moment, Yilo understood one thing: no truth found on the ground could be trusted. If he wanted to know what the Tree really was, he had to see Alpha from outside.

He chose the night of the Great Illumination Festival. While the city gathered in light and prayer, the guard is at its weakest. Yilo slipped past the checkpoints and stole a craft small enough to launch alone.

Instead of fear he anticipated, he felt joyful. Then he looked back.

The Illumination Festival helped a lot, as he could see all the places with light were dominated by roots. However, as the spaceship elevated, a pattern emerged from the twisted vegetation. It was a face. Yilo felt like his throat was seized. Slowly, he turned around, trying not to making eye contact with Alpha.

He believed that he could not go back. But he needed fuel and food. The nearest habitable world was Beta.

Beta is less advanced, but it’s the ideal place for Yilo. Their own Revelation Tree had been in sleep for centuries. No religion and no abnormal feeling in his life for the first time. Yilo thought he belonged here.

He stayed only a few days.

At dawn on the seventh morning, screams waked him up. Blood flowed on the street like a river. Thorn-covered creature walked through the city and killed the residence with an efficiency.

Yilo ran for his ship. By the time he reached the sky, loud sound had spread from the city below. The Beta’s Tree’s branches were fetching for him and trying to shoot him down. He escaped only because Alpha’s craft was faster.

There was nowhere left to go for him. The correctness he had been chasing for was deadly. The space is too large and lonely. After all, he was only 18. He returned home.

He expected prison, even execution. However, priests summoned him and led him into the central room of the greatest temple, where the sacred main root located. Then the Tree spoke with no sound, but with meaning directly into his mind.

The planets were alive and the Trees were their will. Matter within the galaxy was limited. To survive, a Tree had to conquer the other planets. Human are bred for colonization and gathering resource. But they might learn to rebel, to leave, to carry away the precious matter. Therefore, religion was necessary to tie these livestock. And these roots distributed were used to supervise them.

As for Beta, it was just a reset because people there were unqualified and the Tree had consumed its patience. The good news was Alpha was content with them now.

Everything fit. Yilo finally found truth he was desperate for. “But if a system was born with the most fundamental flaws, wouldn’t it come to an end inevitably?”  He thought about it but didn’t ask.

“Are you trying to engage me in your plan?” he asked. He imagined a secret order, the few served directly for the Tree. He imagined that seeing the truth had made him different. Because it seemed there were nothing better than that.

The answer was simple -- No. Nothing about his fault. The Tree simply didn’t care about an ant. The Tree had no need of him. It had not summoned him because he was special. It had watched him just for fun. There were thousands of people in the history thinking they were special in countless loop. That was all. He would be returned to ordinary life and allowed to do whatever he chose, because individual unrest meant nothing at this scale.

When Yilo left the temple, the same people still knelt beside the same roots under the same desert sun.

Nothing had changed.
