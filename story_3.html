Quiet, curious 17-year-old mechanic Laura Voss who always wears a grease-stained jumpsuit, with short silver-gray hair streaked faint blue from years of handling starship coolant, leaned over the attrited control panel of her beat-up starship The Wanderer, pale blue eyes flaring with faint light as she listened to the vessel's tired, rumbling thoughts. The cockpit reeked of burnt wiring and stale recycled air, the hull groaning as it drifted through the jagged debris field of the Ashes Star Domain, where her father's final massage had cut out five years prior. This sci-fi follows her hunt for a legend that could either reunite her with her missing father, or leave her another forgotten ghost in the endless void.

Laura is chasing the Star-Core Engine, a legendary sentient drive capable of bending time and space, because she believes it holds the only clue to her father's disappearance, as well as the power to repair her failing ship and explore the farthest corners of the galaxy. The debris field is littered with derelict warships that flood her mind with their violent, dying memories, and the Engine's last known coordinates lie deep within a gravity well that has torn every approaching vessel to shreds. Laura pried a still-functional navigation core from a crashed scout frigate, her father's dented wrench tight in her grip as she experienced the vessel's painful death memories. She wired the core into The Wanderer's console, holding her breath as she begged the machine to reveal the Star-Core Engine's exact, unmodified coordinates. It didn't have the coordinates and the core's memory banks had been fried by the gravity well's radiation, spitting out garbled data that sent The Wanderer lurching toward a field of sharp hull debris. She slammed the emergency reverse thruster with all her strength, her hands shaking as she wrenched the steering column to narrowly avoid a collision that would have split her ship open.The Wanderer still took a hit, its life support system sputtering as the vessel whispered its quiet fear of falling apart into Laura's mind.

Laura anchored The Wanderer to a massive, half-shattered sentinel cruiser, the only vessel in the field whose thoughts hadn't yet dissolved in her mind.She pressed her palms to the cruiser's frigid, scarred bulkhead, her eyes glowing bright blue as she begged the ancient machine to share what it knew of the Star-Core Engine and her father's ship. Yes, but the cruiser only shared fragmented memories of the Engine's brilliant light vanishing into the gravity well, along with a severe warning that the well would gobble up any machine that tried to cross its threshold. Before she could pull away, the cruiser's centuries-long panic seeped into her mind, leaving her dizzy and disoriented as The Wanderer's anchor line snapped free. She scrambled back to the cockpit, managing to re-stabilize the ship only after it had drifted hundreds of meters closer to the gravity well's inescapable event horizon.

When Lira collapsed back into her pilot's seat, her father's wrench pressed to her chest, she finally heard the quiet, familiar hum she'd been chasing for years-hidden deep within The Wanderer's core, her father had installed a fragment of the Star-Core Engine before he left, leaving her a map to his location woven into the ship's very soul. Her quiet, curious resolve never wavered, even as the gravity well loomed ahead, because she finally had the clue she'd spent half her life searching for.The cockpit, once filled with the dread of the void, now overflowed with quiet hope, The Wanderer's thoughts aligning with her own as they prepared to dive into the unknown together.
