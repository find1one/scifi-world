Godbuilder

In the industrial city of Grey Harbor, old gods have been forgotten and replaced by engines, factories, and machines. But beneath the smoke and steel, a secret organization called The Builders is trying to create a new god—not through faith, but through technology. White Luo, once a quiet boy, is turned into an experimental vessel meant to contain the stolen faith of the city. As his body becomes less human and the voices of Grey Harbor flood into his mind, he discovers the truth behind The Builders' plan. Before dawn, he must decide whether to become the god they designed—or return the city's faith to where it truly belongs.

White Luo, a quiet, meticulous and brave boy, slipped through the alleys of Grey Harbor with a rusty key in his sleeve.

Steam hissed from the massive tubes over his head, and the engine in the center of Grey Harbor pumped like the beating of a heart. Along the riverside, the mingled sounds of flowing water, factory machines and people's whispers seemed to carry a heavy history of Grey Harbor, meanwhile, the vague and distant legend of the River God.

In Grey Harbor, people used to leave fruits or joss sticks beside the river during May. They believed there was an ancient god living in the river, who would bring them peace and protect the city from storms. However, countless factories and huge smokestacks rose across this land, burying the old legend by the magic of industrial progress. The god may not have disappeared, but fewer and fewer people knew how to talk to him.

White Luo remembered.

Ten years ago, White Luo was secretly taken to a mysterious underground organization by a group of people who claimed to be The Builders. They told White Luo: "The world is changing. The age of prayer is ending. There is no more need for human to believe in an imaginary god made of water, fog or prayer. It needs something stronger, something precise, something designed and built to control and manage everything."

"A god," a thin man in the front said, eyes glistening with metallic luster, "God should exist for long time, not dependent on faith."

White Luo lost his name that night. He was only known as The Ten.

The transformation of his body began with the spine. The back was cut open, and a bronze wire wrapped in metal replaced the long bone. Intense pain swept over the entire body. Screams from the young boy filled the whole room, but deep under the ground, no one cared enough to pity him.

Pain can be normal in following days.

"He is promising," one doctor spoke to others, "other samples either got mad, or died. Only he survived."

Months passed. White Luo always appeared compliant, but inside his heart, the fire of uncovering the purpose of The Builders never extinguished.

One day, when the guards changed their shifts, he moved.

Climbing the long staircase, White Luo returned to the ground level of Grey Harbor, and hurried straightly to the core archive room of The Builder. Using a rusty key in his sleeve stolen from a careless guard, he opened the heavy metal door.

Rows of iron cabinets held old experiment records and designs, with the dim light cast upon them, creating a chilling sense of solemnity.

After searching through several files, White Luo found his own record. He removed layers of tangled wires, opened the kraft bag, and took out the papers full of hand-writing words.

"... After current modification, his body has been tested to have enough strength to contain a part of 'Faith', and next, we will ..."

A sudden and unexpected noise hit White Luo's back. The door was kicked open. These researchers have noticed his missing and followed the trail all the way here.

White Luo was forced back to that icy metal bed. Next came physical punishment.

However, he got a general idea for what The Builders wanted to do: they were trying to use technology to contain the stolen faith of Grey Harbor and turn him into a new River God.

Second stage of modification was advanced. Researchers put nine silver coins near his heart, not heart, now there was only a micro-engine, supplying energy for the whole designed body. Each coin contained certain fragment - legend, instruction, stories, memory......

Late night, White Luo put his hand on the wall.

He could hear them.

Initially voices. The sound of current flowing through the wires. The sound of rats in the sewer. Gradually, it turned to arguments, prayers, laughter, alarm sounds, bird songs and river sounds. Finally came the ancient legends, the folk tales passed down, the history records on the temple stones...... All these fragments were colliding and rushing to him.

White Luo fainted. In a hazy moment, he heard someone entered and saying something joyfully.

He noticed that there was little time left. He needed to have second try.

One early morning, White Luo slipped out again. He ran toward the engine in the center of the Grey Harbor, where the ritual machines were hidden.

He believed that if he could destroy the machine before dawn, he might stop the Builders from binding him to the city forever.

When he arrived the center region, he found that the machine was already awake, glowing blue light and blowing steam outward.

White Luo forced himself to connect to it, hoping to shut it down from within, and for one brief moment he heard everything - the whispers of workers, the cries of children, and the deep, ancient voice of the River God beneath the city.

Yet he also realized a cruel truth: he could no longer return to being fully human, and if fate demanded that he became a god, would he be the one made for the invasion and plundering?

White Luo made his choice. He sent the stolen Faith surging back through the wires, through the engine, and into the river beneath the city.

The engine collapsed, and Grey Harbor fell silent for a minute.

No one had seen White Luo again.

Yet every May, fruits or joss sticks still appeared beside the river. The people of Grey Harbor began to whisper that maybe the River God had not vanished at all, and he had returned, perhaps, under another name.
